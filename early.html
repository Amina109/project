EARLY MODERN PERIOD(1500CE-1800CE)

Some of the thorough and analyzed facts about the Early Modern Period are as follows:

Major events:

  • Renaissance (14th–17th century) Sparked a revival in art, science, and culture.
  • Reformation (1517) Led by Martin Luther, challenging the Catholic Church and beginning the rise of Protestantism.
  • Age of Exploration (15th–17th centuries) Including the voyages of Christopher Columbus (1492) and Vasco da Gama, which connected Europe with the Americas, Africa, and Asia.
  • Scientific Revolution (16th–18th centuries) Brought profound changes in scientific thought with figures like Copernicus, Galileo, and Newton.
  • English Civil War (1642–1651)leading to the establishment of a republic under Oliver Cromwell.
  • American Revolution (1775–1783)which resulted in the independence of the United States.
  • French Revolution (1789–1799)Overthrowing the monarchy and establishing the First French Republic.

Key Figures:

  • Martin Luther (1483–1546): Leader of the Protestant Reformation.
  • Nicolaus Copernicus (1473–1543):Astronomer who developed the heliocentric theory.
  • Galileo Galilei (1564–1642): Physicist and astronomer who supported the heliocentric theory.
  • Isaac Newton (1642–1727): Mathematician and physicist who formulated the laws of motion and universal gravitation.
  • Christopher Columbus (1451–1506): Explorer who opened up the Americas to European colonization.

Cultural Developments:

  • Renaissance:Flourishing of art, literature, and humanism with figures like Leonardo da Vinci and Shakespeare.
  • Baroque Art (17th century): Known for grandeur and emotion, with artists like Rembrandt and Caravaggio.
  • Enlightenment (18th century): Focused on reason, individualism, and skepticism of authority, with philosophers like Voltaire and Rousseau.

Social Changes:

  • The Protestant Reformation: Challenged the Catholic Church's power and led to new Christian denominations.
  • Urbanization:Movement to cities due to economic changes and the rise of trade.

Economic Developments:

  • Mercantilism: Focused on accumulating wealth through trade and colonial expansion.
  • Rise of Capitalism: The establishment of joint-stock companies, like the Dutch East India Company (1602).
  • Colonial Economies: Relied on agriculture, resources, and slave labor in the Americas and Africa.

Environmental Factors:

  • Columbian Exchange:Exchange of goods, animals, and diseases between the Old and New Worlds.
  • Exploitation of natural resources:Deforestation and environmental changes due to European colonialism.

Technological Advancements:

  • Printing Press: Revolutionized the spread of knowledge (c. 1440, by Johannes Gutenberg).
  • Navigational Tools:Compass, astrolabe, and improved shipbuilding for global exploration.
  • Microscope and Telescope:Revolutionized science, especially astronomy and biology.
